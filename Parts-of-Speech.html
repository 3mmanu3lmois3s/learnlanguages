Parts of Speech

Noun

Person, Place or Thing

Person: Boy, Girl, Teacher
Place: School, Park, Home
Thing: Apple, Backpack, Pencil

Pronoun

Takes the Place of a Noun and May Replace a Name

I, He, She, You
We, They, It, Me, Us

Verb

Describes an Action or a State of Being

Action: Run, Walk, Talk, Think
State of Being: Is, Was, Am, Are

Adverb

Tells How, When, Where, or To What Extent

How: Perfectly
When: Yesterday
Where: Here
Extent: Completely

Adjective

Describes a Noun or Pronoun

Happy, More, Healthy, This
Seven, That

Article

Comes Before a Noun in a Sentence and is a Special Kind of Adjective

A: A Boy, A Girl, A Teacher
An: An Ant, An Orange, An Airplane
The: The Apple, The Pencil, The Cat

Preposition

Used with a Noun or Pronoun to Form a Phrase

At: At Home
From: From There
By: By the Lake
With: With Us

Conjunction

Joins Words and Groups of Words Within a Sentence

And, But, Or
Also, Either, Except

Interjection

Strong Feeling or Reaction

Uh-Oh!, Hey!
Yea!, Wow!, Oh-No!
